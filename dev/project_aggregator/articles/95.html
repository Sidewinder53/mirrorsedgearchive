Mirror's Edge Catalyst Delayed

Back in October when Electronic Arts (EA) announced the delay of the much-awaited reboot, Mirror's Edge Catalyst, game developer EA DICE reasoned that they plan to make this ambitious video game project to achieve "that vision and delivering the best possible experience is equally inspiring and challenging," as posted on the official website of Mirror's Edge.

"And it takes time," the game developer admitted.

Explaining the reason for the delay, Mirror's Edge Catalyst senior producer Sara Jansson stated that the game developers are targeting to "create a vast city to explore, full of stunning vistas, majestic skyscrapers, and mysterious tunnels. We're building the most immersive action-adventure game with fluid movement and combat in first person. Mirror's Edge Catalyst will be an all-new playground with a deeper and more engaging story - and we want to accomplish all these goals to the highest quality possible."

"So we've made the decision to bring Mirror's Edge to the world starting May 24, 2016," Jansson added.

Mirror's Edge Catalyst Massive City of Glass

And here's the biggest revelation for Mirror's Edge Catalyst - "We will use these few extra months to make sure that when you step into the massive City of Glass and experience the rise of Faith, it's as entertaining, impressive, and memorable as it can be," Jansson revealed.

Later, Jansson posted on her Twitter account this announcement,

What Can We Expect on Mirror's Edge Catalyst?

As Michael Thomsen wrote on Forbes.com, "The gunplay in the first Mirror's Edge was criticized for being shallow and fidgety," but then relating to what he experienced in playing the reboot, "With Catalyst, DICE seems to have compensated for the absence of guns by making Faith's hand-to-hand power dramatically more powerful."

Thomsen added, "Catalyst feels both smoother and more automated; a quality that's intensified by the absence of tactical options with guns.

Sam Loveridge wrote on Trusted Reviews that "Faith has a new trick up her sleeve. Well, rather on her sleeve. Faith's glove has been upgraded with some technological advancements, which you can equip through a progression system upgrade."

Loveridge explained, "It's not about giving Faith fake abilities though, it's about enhancing the ones that she's already got - jumping further, running faster and climbing higher. Initially, you won't be able to move around the world as seamlessly as you'd like. Some ledges and platforms will be simply outside of Faith's reach, but as you progress through the levels you'll get new gadgets to make you move in new ways."

Mirror's Edge Catalyst is scheduled for release May 24, 2016 on Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One platforms.
